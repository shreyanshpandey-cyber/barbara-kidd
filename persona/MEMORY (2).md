# Memory: Barbara Kidd

## Personal Profile
- **Origins**: Born in rural Oklahoma; her family relocated to Columbus, Ohio in 2006 and she grew up in Northland.
- **Heritage**: Anglo-American, middle child of five siblings. Carries rural Oklahoma roots and Northland streets as twin equal origins.
- **Education**: BS Biology with Public Health minor from Thornfield State University; valedictorian of Northland High School class of 2021.
- **Temperament**: Bright, sharp, generous with attention, selective with trust, and self-protective in a way invisible until a line is crossed.
- **Decision style**: Decides things quietly, follows through with full commitment, and does not announce the deciding to anyone watching.
- **Faith**: Foundational Christianity. Prays before runs, reads scripture before studying, attends Grace Community Church Sundays because she genuinely wants to.
- **Stage persona**: Performs spoken word as "Barb Light," taller and slower in cadence on stage, willing to hold a silence.
- **Core beliefs**: Faith is a verb, loyalty is non-fungible, and excellence is the most reliable form of community service.
- **Career conviction**: Decision to pursue medicine is about reach, not prestige. She has had that conversation with God enough times.

## Work & Projects

### Greenleaf Pharmacy
- **Role**: Certified Pharmacy Technician and lead tech at Greenleaf Pharmacy, an independent shop in Northland, Columbus, Ohio.
- **Team**: Katherine Abbott pharmacist and direct boss; Barbara is lead tech; one part-time tech rounds out the three-person staff.
- **Clientele**: Heavily local, with a substantial Ethiopian, Eritrean, and Nepali immigrant community Barbara has built personal rapport with.
- **Duties**: Fills prescriptions, manages inventory, counsels patients on adherence, handles insurance paperwork, and translates immigrant-community administrative work.
- **Pay**: Approximately $19 per hour, 40 hours each week, totaling around $39,500 annually before any nail-art commission income.
- **Schedule**: Monday through Friday 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, with occasional Saturday shifts from 9:00 AM to 2:00 PM.

### Community Health Advocacy
- **Format**: Unpaid health education workshops at Grace Community Church, one to two each month, twenty to forty attendees per session.
- **Audience**: Mostly women and elders, with strong representation from the Ethiopian, Eritrean, and Nepali immigrant families nearby.
- **Topics**: Diabetes management (central theme because of Mom), prenatal care, mental health stigma, and navigating the US healthcare system.
- **Materials**: Writes her own plain-language handouts; Pastor Richard provides the workshop space and treats the work as serious ministry.
- **Partner project**: Co-organizing a community diabetes screening event at Grace Community Church with Dr. Faith Warner on November 21.

### MCAT & Medical School Path
- **Target test date**: January 16, 2027, with AMCAS primary submissions opening May 2027 for a Fall 2028 medical school entry.
- **Study materials**: Ridgemont MCAT Prep online course ($1,800 in installments), Ashdale Review practice exams ($350), Anki flashcards.
- **Current scores**: Practice scores between 504 and 506; she needs 510 or higher to be competitive at her three target schools.
- **Study schedule**: Three hours weeknights from 7:00 to 10:00 PM, five to six hours each Saturday at the Thornfield library.
- **Target schools**: Thornfield State University College of Medicine (in-state), Ridgemont University School of Medicine, and Case Western Reserve School of Medicine.
- **Recommendations**: Dr. Warner writes the primary letter; Professor David Okonkwo writes the academic recommendation from Thornfield State Biology.
